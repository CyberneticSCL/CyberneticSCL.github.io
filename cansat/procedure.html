The Procedure for building our CanSat was as follows

Structure

  • To begin, we created a frame on which to mount the electronics needed to operate the satellite. This was done taking copper-plated composite fiber plate and cutting two circles and a rectangular piece. These pieces were soldered together to form the shape of a soda can.
  • Holes were then drilled in the frame to allow passage of wires through the plate. Holes were then drilled for the bolts needed to hold the circuit-boards in place. 
  • A loop was attached to the top of the structure to hold the parachute
  • Holes were drilled in the top of the can to allow the radio antenna and volume know to stick out
  • Vector boards were cut to appropriate size to fit into the CanSat.
  • A sheet of plastic was cut into a circle and threaded to create a drawstring pouch held shut by a nylon wire
  • A pepsi can was cut to allow the antenna, volume knob and light sensor to protrude from the vehicle, as well as to allow access to the on/off switch for the 12V power supply.
  • A copper wire loop was soldered to the bottom of the top circle of the frame from which to hang the payload

Electronics

  • The circuit design was laid out on paper and the appropriate positions found on the vector board.
  • chip sockets were soldered in the appropriate locations
  • 12V and 5V power and ground busses were laid out on the board
  • The resisters, capacitors, osciallators, transistors, nicrome wire, regulator and diodes were soldered to the board.
  • The 6 555 chips, the voltage inverter and the DTMF chip were set into the board.
  • The connections depicted in the circuit diagram were made to the chips and components using small gauge wire
  • The speaker output from the radio was soldered to the input of the DTMF chip
  • The output from the 555 chips and transistors was connected to the mike in and PTT connections on the radio
  • The batteries were connected to the power switch and a line from the 6V source connected to the radio. A line from the 12V source was connected to the regulator on the circuit board

Assembly

  • Once the circuit boards were prepared, the diassembled radio cicuit board was first mounted to the frame using nuts, bolts and spacers.
  • The circuit boards were then mounted to the opposite side of the frame as the radio
  • The battery assembly was mounted to the bottom of the frame
  • The nylon wire holding the payload was threaded through the copper loop at the top of the frame and twisted about the nicrome wire before tying both ends together
  • The CanSat was now ready for deployment
